01 What is a Blockchain in GameFi?

Think of a blockchain like a digital ledger or a shared notebook that everyone can see but no single person can erase or alter. Once information (like a transaction) is written into it, it's there forever.

  • Decentralisation: No central authority (like a bank) controls it. It's maintained by a global network of computers.
  • Transparency: Anyone can view the transactions on public block explorers.
  • Immutability: Data cannot be changed once confirmed.
02 What is the TON Blockchain (The Open Network)?

TON is a fast, highly scalable blockchain originally designed by the team behind Telegram. It was built to process millions of transactions per second, making it incredibly well-suited for TON GameFi, social apps, and micro-transactions.

05 What is a DEX on TON? How Swaps Work

A DEX (Decentralised Exchange) like STON.fi is a platform that allows users to trade cryptocurrencies directly with one another without needing a centralized middleman (like a bank or traditional exchange).

Liquidity Pools

Instead of order books, DEXs use liquidity pools. These are massive pots of paired tokens (like TON and $TSUN) locked in a smart contract. When you buy $TSUN with TON, you are adding TON to the pool and taking $TSUN out. This changes the ratio and determines the price.

Slippage

The difference between the expected price of a trade and the executed price. Slippage happens in fast-moving markets when the price shifts while your transaction is processing.

Gas Fees

A tiny fractional cost paid in TON to process and validate your transaction on the network.

10 TON & GameFi Glossary
Airdrop

A distribution of cryptocurrency tokens to a large number of wallet addresses, often for free or as a reward for engaging in community tasks.

DYOR

"Do Your Own Research". A standard crypto motto reminding users not to blindly trust what others say and verify facts themselves.

Gas Fee

A small network transaction fee paid to validators to process operations on the blockchain. On TON, this is paid in TON coin.

Rug Pull

A scam where developers abandon a project and run away with investors' funds, typically by draining the liquidity pool. Liquidity locks can reduce some risks but do not eliminate all risk.

Smart Contract

Self-executing code on the blockchain that automatically runs when predetermined conditions are met. Jettons (tokens) on TON are controlled by smart contracts.
